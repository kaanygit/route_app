Ulvi Sultan Mosque is an essential place of worship that reflects Konya's historical and cultural fabric. Constructed in 1917, the mosque not only embodies the unique characteristics of Ottoman architecture but also incorporates influences from structures of the Republican era. Built with cut stone and brick, the mosque's architectural design exhibits remarkable symmetry and balance. The decorations and stained glass within its interior reflect the artistic understanding of the period, while the mosque's courtyard provides a spacious area, serving as a community gathering point. Beyond being a place of worship, Ulvi Sultan Mosque offers visitors a profound experience through its architectural aesthetics, historical significance, and social functionality.
